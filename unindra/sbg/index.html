🎰 How BINGO Works
🎯 The goal: Be the first player to fill a complete line of sounds on your card.
A line = any full row →, column ↓, or diagonal ↗↘.
🃏
1 — Get your card Your card has a grid of English sound symbols (like /ʃ/, /æ/, /iː/). Every player gets a different random card — same sounds, different positions.
📢
2 — The caller says a word The teacher (or computer in Solo) reads a word aloud — like "ship". Listen carefully.
🧠
3 — Identify the sound "ship" starts with the /ʃ/ sound (SH). That's the sound you're looking for on your card. This is the English part — you have to think!
✅
4 — Mark it (ONLY if called!) If /ʃ/ is on your card → tap it to mark it.
⚠️ You can ONLY mark a sound that was just called. You cannot mark random squares — that's cheating and the game won't accept it!
🎰
5 — Shout BINGO! Complete a full line and press BINGO! The game verifies your marked squares match what was actually called. First correct BINGO wins!

Three ways to play
📱 Digital Teacher calls on projector, tap sounds as called, then mark your card
📺 Single One projector screen, everyone plays verbally
🖨️ Print Paper bingo cards + digital caller, mark with pencil
🎲 A note on luck: BINGO is a game of chance — sounds are called randomly and every card is shuffled differently. Listening sharp and recognising sounds quickly is the skill part. Winning a round, though, is the luck part. Practice the sounds, enjoy the game, and don't take a loss personally — the draw decides each round.

No money, no prizes, no stakes — just sound practice with a playful twist.

Marking Mode
🔒 Strict marking
Students must tap the called sound before the next call — miss it and it's gone. Off = any called sound stays tappable until marked.
💡 Highlight tappable cell
Glows the matching cell on the student's card so they know where to tap. Off = students must identify the sound on their own (harder, better practice).
